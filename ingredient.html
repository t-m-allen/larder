Wild
Garlic

Found in ancient woodland and shaded river banks from late March to May. Its broad, fragrant leaves carry the clean, green bite of garlic without the heat.

Peak: April – May British Woodland Foraged

Hover to see in colour — Allium ursinum, British Woodland, April

In Season Mar – May
Where to Find Woodland floors
Pairs With Butter, lemon, pasta
Intensity Mild — Medium

Selected Preparations

Sorted by simplicity
01

Wild Garlic Pesto

10 mins / No cook

Raw leaves blended with toasted pine nuts, aged parmesan and cold-pressed rapeseed oil. Vivid green. Keeps for a week in the fridge.

arrow_forward
02

Jersey Royal & Wild Garlic Soup

15 mins prep / 25 mins cook

New-season Jersey Royals simmered in chicken stock, finished with a generous handful of wild garlic leaves stirred in at the last moment.

arrow_forward
03

Wildgarlic Butter

10 mins / Refrigerate overnight

Leaves macerated in salted cultured butter, rolled in parchment and frozen in logs. Use to finish grilled fish, steak or melt over new potatoes.
