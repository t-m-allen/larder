Recipe No. 001 / Spring Forage

Wild
Garlic
Pesto

Raw and vivid. A no-cook method that preserves the volatile aromatics of foraged leaves. Ready in under ten minutes.

Prep Time 10 Minutes
Total Time 10 Minutes
Yield 01 Jar / 250ml
Difficulty Simple

The Larder

  • Wild Garlic Leaves 100g
  • Pine Nuts, toasted 50g
  • Aged Parmesan 60g
  • Cold-pressed Rapeseed Oil 120ml
  • Maldon Sea Salt Generous pinch
  • Lemon Juice ½ lemon
Forager's Note

"Pick only from areas free of dog traffic. Confirm identification by smell — if it doesn't smell of garlic, do not use it."

The Method

01

Prepare the Leaves

Wash the wild garlic thoroughly under cold running water. Dry well — excess moisture will dilute the pesto and shorten its shelf life. Remove any yellowed or damaged leaves.

02

Build the Emulsion

Add the leaves, toasted pine nuts and lemon juice to a food processor. Pulse roughly five times — you want texture, not a smooth purée. With the motor running, drizzle in the rapeseed oil slowly until emulsified.

03

Finish & Season

Fold in the grated parmesan by hand with a spatula — do not blitz it in. Season aggressively with Maldon salt, taste, and adjust with more lemon if the garlic flavour feels heavy. Transfer to a sterilised jar, seal with a thin layer of oil.

04

Store & Serve

Refrigerate immediately. Use within seven days. Excellent tossed through fresh pasta with pecorino, spread on sourdough with ricotta, or stirred into a spring vegetable broth at the last moment before serving.
